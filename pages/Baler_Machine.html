Types of Balers All State Ships

We ship all kinds of balers to all regions of the country.

Vertical balers

Vertical balers are used to compress and pack the hay or the grass in the vertical direction. The side of the machinery comprises the compressed chamber. ASTSAT has brief knowledge and experience of their shipping.

Horizontal baler

Horizontal balers work by compressing the grass or the hay in the horizontal direction. These balers are larger than the vertical balers and can easily manage a large load volume. So, their shipping needs more consideration than that of vertical balers. We offer expert-level shipping services for this farm equipment. 

Round baler

Cylindrical or round-shaped balers can collect a large quantity of crop waste or grass. They are often used to gather hay straw and green grass. Timely shipping of round balers is an important concern for farmers and workers who collect the larger waste on farms. Our 5-star shipping service is the best to timely shipping them at your desired location. 

Square balers

The square balers can create rectangular bales. They’re used to pack denser crops that require high stacking. These balers can also be used to control mushroom cultivation, soil erosion, and biomass fuel. Contact us to ship them to any location.

How Balers are Being Shipped

We ship these balers in different positions according to their dimensions and weight. Some vertical balers are shipped on their sides, and some are shipped standing. Some balers are transported by removing their cylinders, and some are transported in the same condition as they are ready to work. These balers are shipped in different conditions depending on their size, shape, and dimension, but you don’t need to worry all about that and start your shipping journey with us for a memorable shipping experience.

1. Shipping a baler on its sides

The balers being shipped on flatbed trailers are placed on their sides due to the shape of the flatbed trailer, which is open from its sides and has no roof. Transporting a baler in a standing position on a flatbed is difficult, but All State uses the right approach to safety for the secure movement of this equipment.

2. Shipping a baler upwards

Bailers with a large width or huge dimensions. Vertical balers like M60MD, M42HD, or any other similar model are being shipped in a standing position with their cylinder dropped to acquire more space on the trailer. Suppose you are shipping such a baler with a non-removable cylinder.

Cost to Ship a Baler

The shipping cost of a baler for less than 500 miles is $4$ to $5$, and if you want baler shipping for more than 500 miles, the minimum cost will be $1.5$ to $3$. Its cost is affected by accessibility, size and weight, permit fee required, and the region of the transportation.

Guideline for Customers to Carefully Consider the Baler Dimension

The most commonly used trailer for hauling the baler is the step-desk trailer. The large balers are about 9 feet in height, and the transportation of such large balers is easily carried out by the step deck trailer. If you plan to transport a baler of about 8.5 feet, it will be shipped in a flatbed trailer. The average weight of the large baler is 1000 lbs. and no trailer is allowed to carry more than this load. If the size of the balers is small, then more than one trailer at a time, but in the case of an oversized baler, you need to inform us already if you want to ship more than one at a time. In that case, you must contact the shipping companies' experts at All State to State Auto Transport to get it properly loaded upwards to avoid any problems during its shipping.
