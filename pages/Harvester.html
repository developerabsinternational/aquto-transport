How to prepare the Harvester before Shipping

The important points that must be considered before shipping your harvester are:

  • Understanding the size and dimension of your harvester is the first step that needs special attention in its preparation for shipping. This will help you to understand the legal considerations for moving your harvester to the respective areas.
  • Clean the dirt and debris and ensure the agricultural residues have been cleaned properly before shipping.
  • Inspect the harvester properly to check for any wear and tears that already exist.
  • Thoroughly wash all the parts and check that the tie-down parts are properly washed, cleaned, and dried before hauling.
  • Protect your harvester from rust by applying any anti-rusting component.
  • Park your harvester in the open location to be loaded easily without any damage.

Types of Harvesters We Transport

Combined harvester

It is the most commonly and widely used harvester. These harvesters provide the facility of thrashers and harvesters with their adjustable features.

Sugarcane harvested harvester

This harvester is specifically designed to harvest sugarcane. Its blades do not damage the sugarcane, and these harvesters initially refine the sugarcane.

Robotic harvestane harvested harvester

This harvester can collect various fruits and vegetables. There are two types of robotic harvesters.

Repair binder

This is a small harvester used to prevent grain loss during harvesting. Small crops like rice, wheat, barley, and maize are harvested using a repair binder to ensure a safe harvesting process without any loss or damage to the crop.

Types of Trucks We Used for Harvester Shipping

There are different types of harvesters, and the process of shipping harvesters is carried out on different trailers, considering their shape, size, and weight. However, we ship all kinds of Harvesters.

Flatbed trailers

It is the most specialized and well-designed trailer for heavy and oversized loads. These trailers have a flat surface and are open from all sides. This ensures the easy loading and unloading of the harvesters and safe time and money.

Lowboys

The lowboy trailer makes shipping a harvester easy due to its hydraulic system. The trailer's height is also low, and the driver easily drives the harvester to load into it.

Specialized haulers

These trailers are well-designed to manage the issues faced while shipping oversized and overweight harvesters. Due to proper and adjustable fitting options, moving your harvester in a specialized trailer is cost-effective.
